George Mason Honors College RTOC

During the fall semester of 2013, I worked with the George Mason University Honors College as a part of the Research, Technology, and Online Community (RTOC) initiative with the goal of developing online interactive tools to support first year students engaging in complex research processes.

I led the development of Research Questions which assists students new to the academic research process. Specifically, the interactive web service allows students to anonymously submit their research questions for community peer-review. The academic and critical conversation is facilitated by a rating and commenting system. A user-logging system adds accountability to the student discussion, and allows faculty and mentors to moderate the service.

I additionally worked on a restoration and modernization project called Reading Scholarly Articles which updated the NCSU library website Anatomy of a Scholarly Article.
